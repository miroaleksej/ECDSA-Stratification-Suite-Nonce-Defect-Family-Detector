## 2. Theoretical Framework

### 2.1 Standard ECDSA Scalar Algebra
All scalar operations are defined within the finite field $\mathbb{Z}_n$, where $n$ is the order of the subgroup generated by the base point $G$.

* **Key Generation:** $Q = d \cdot G$, where $d \in [1, n-1]$ is the private key.
* **Signature Generation:** For a message hash $z$ and a unique secret nonce $k$:
  1. $R = k \cdot G = (x_R, y_R)$ 
  2. $r = x_R \pmod n$ 
  3. $s = k^{-1}(z + r \cdot d) \pmod n$

### 2.2 Phase Coordinate Transformation $(u_r, u_z)$
To analyze structural concentrations, the signature is projected into **phase coordinates** derived from the verification equation:
$$w = s^{-1} \pmod n$$
$$u_z = z \cdot w \pmod n$$
$$u_r = r \cdot w \pmod n$$

The geometric verification condition is then represented as a point recovery on the curve:
$$R' = u_z \cdot G + u_r \cdot Q$$ 
$$\text{Valid if: } x(R') \pmod n = r$$
